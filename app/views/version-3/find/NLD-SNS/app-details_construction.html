Building services design engineer

This is an apprenticeship standard developed by a group of employers. It sets out what the apprentice needs to achieve and how they will be assessed.

  • Save this apprenticeship

Summary of this apprenticeship standard

Overview of role

A building services design engineer will manage a team of engineers and technicians and will collaborate with other construction professionals to design the various services found in buildings and infrastructure projects.

Level

6 (equivalent to bachelor's degree)

Training costs

Maximum total cost:£3,000 per apprentice

Government will pay £2,700 per apprentice

Costs to you:£300 per apprentice

If your pay bill is more than £3 million a year, the cost of training will be different

Typical length

66 months

Entry requirements

Set by employer. Candidates will usually have 1 of the following:

level 2 maths and English, ideally as part of 5 GCSEs grades A to C equivalent qualifications or prior experience

What apprentices will learn

  • comparing and selecting methods for equipment sizing in power systems
  • assessing the suitability of computational fluid dynamics for understanding thermal and ventilation dynamic characteristics in complex scenarios
  • promoting the continuous improvement of the design of building services systems and components.
  • managing the planning, budgeting and organisation of tasks, people and resources through the use of appropriate management systems
  • managing teams and developing staff to meet changing technical and managerial needs
  • communicating effectively through reports, drawings, specifications, presentations and discussions with both technical and non-technical people
  • managing and applying safe systems of work including taking responsibility for own obligations for health, safety and welfare issues, assessing and controlling risk, working with health, safety and welfare legislation and best practice.
  • carry out and record the continuing professional development necessary to maintain and enhance knowledge and competence as a building services design engineer

Qualifications

Apprentices will take a range of professional qualifications, as set out in the apprenticeship standard.

Candidates who don’t have English and maths at grace C or above (or equivalent) will have to achieve this level before the end of their apprenticeship

Professional registration

None specified

Apprenticeship status explained

Apprenticeship training is scrutinised and approved by Government before being made available. Below is an explanation of the different stages leading up to approval apprenticeship training.

Ready for delivery (standard and assessment plan approved)

  • Means it is approved by SFA
  • Does not mean it has providers offering the course
  • Newly approved courses may not have providers ready to deliver it.
  • The problem here is the terminology is focussed around the provider (ready for delivery, instead of has providers)

Standard in development

  • Means it has not been approved yet
  • could be in early development maybe already in the approval process
  • trial blazers are responsible/point of contact at this point to find out more.
  • In development standards levels are subject to change
  • No assessment plan is complete at this point

Standard approved

  • Means the standard has been approved
  • But the assessment plan for the standard has not
  • trial blazers are responsible/point of contact at this point to find out more.
